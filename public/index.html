How to use UTM Maestro

UTM Maestro helps you create, manage, and analyze UTM-tagged links across all marketing channels. Every link follows a consistent naming convention (taxonomy) so your analytics stay clean and comparable.

1 Generator

The Generator tab is where you create new UTM links. Fill in the required fields, save the link, and get a trackable URL plus a short link for sharing.

Workflow

  1. Enter the Destination URL — the page you want to send traffic to.
  2. Select or type a Source, Medium, and Campaign. The autocomplete dropdown suggests standard values from our taxonomy (see below).
  3. Optionally expand "More +" to add Content, Author, or a Note.
  4. The UTM URL preview updates live as you type.
  5. Click Save to store the link. A unique short link is generated automatically.
  6. After saving, use Copy UTM, Copy Short Link, or QR ↓ to share the link.

Hover over the ? icon next to each field label for a quick reference of accepted values.

UTM Taxonomy — Naming Convention

All UTM parameters follow a strict naming convention to keep data consistent across campaigns, channels, and team members.

General Rules

  • Everything lowercase, separator is a hyphen (-), no spaces, no umlauts.
  • Values are auto-slugified when saving.

utm_source — Where does the traffic come from?

The platform or sender. Use the same spelling every time.

The channel type. Use values from this fixed list only.

Uses the schema: {type}-{name}-{period}

utm_content — What exactly? (Optional)

Use to differentiate within a campaign. Free-form but keep it consistent.

Examples: variante-a, hero-cta, footer-cta, whitepaper-sap-cloud, bild-1, video-kurz

2 Registry

The Registry is your central library of all saved UTM links.

Features

  • Search & Filter — Use the toolbar to search across all fields, or filter by campaign, source, medium, status, or date range. Hit Enter in any filter field to apply.
  • Sortable Columns — Click any column header to sort ascending, descending, or reset. Works for date, campaign, source, medium, clicks, author, status, sessions, and conversions.
  • Quick Actions per row:
    • 🔗 Copy the short link
    • ⧉ Copy the full UTM URL
    • ⊞ Download a QR code (SVG or PNG)
    • ✎ Edit all fields including the short link slug
    • ⊠ / ↩ Archive or reactivate a link
    • ✕ Permanently delete a link
  • Right-click any cell to quickly copy its value.
  • CSV Export — Download the current (filtered) view as a CSV file.
  • CSV Import — Upload a CSV with columns: campaign, source, medium, destination_url, utm_url (required), plus optional content, created_by, note.

Editing a Link

Click the ✎ icon to open the edit modal. You can change any field, including the short link slug. The UTM URL preview updates live as you edit. Slug changes are validated — duplicates are blocked.

3 Dashboard

The Dashboard combines GA4 analytics with click tracking data to give you a full picture of campaign performance.

Campaigns View

  • Shows all campaign/source/medium combinations with Sessions, Users, Conversions, Conversion Rate, and Clicks (from short link tracking).
  • A green dot indicates that the UTM combination exists in the registry.
  • Click any row to open the detail modal with 5 metric cards (Clicks, Sessions, Conversions, Bounce Rate, Avg. Duration) and two charts (Sessions over time, Clicks over time).

Channels View

A bar chart breaking down sessions by channel (source/medium), giving you a quick visual comparison of which channels drive the most traffic.

Controls

  • Date range — Choose 7 days, 30 days, 90 days, or a custom range.
  • Refresh GA4 — Fetches the latest data from Google Analytics. GA4 data is cached and refreshed automatically every night at 03:00.
  • CSV ↓ — Export the current dashboard data.

4 Short Links & Click Tracking

Every saved link automatically gets a short link like utm.versino.de/a3x9kf. When someone clicks the short link:

  1. The click is logged with a timestamp.
  2. The user is redirected (302) to the full UTM URL.
  3. Google Analytics picks up the UTM parameters on the destination page.

This gives you two independent data points: click counts from the redirect (visible in Registry & Dashboard) and session/conversion data from GA4.

You can customize the slug in the edit modal (e.g. change a3x9kf to b1-cloud). Duplicate slugs are blocked.
